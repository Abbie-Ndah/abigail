    Background

    As a child, I learned about kwashiorkor not from textbooks, but from the harrowing experiences of my community. Before I even set foot in college, I had witnessed the devastating effects of severe hunger and malnutrition, particularly kwashiorkor, which ravaged the children of families, friends, and neighbors. The root of this scourge was a pervasive root rot disease that wreaked havoc on our crops, diminishing both yield and nutritional value. By the time I reached college, I was resolved to not only be the first woman in my community to earn a doctorate but to dedicate my life to studying plant-microbe interactions.

    In spring 2018, I began my research career volunteering as Student Laboratory Assistant in the Biological Science Lab at Kogi State University while in college. Afterwards I did my internship at National Biosafety Management Agency, Abuja. After my undergraduate studies, I worked at Dalhuta Araf Specialist Hospital as a Research Aid. .

    I have completed two projects as an Undergraduate. I am seeking to pursue a graduate program. I wanted to sharpen my research skills and make meaningful contribution to the field of Plant Pathology.
